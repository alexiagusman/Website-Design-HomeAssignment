Dogs

By Kevin

Dogs have an extraordinary sense of smell, with some breeds having up to 300 million smell receptors in their noses.

Dogs have been humans' loyal companions for thousands of years, and their diverse breeds showcase a wide range of sizes, shapes, and abilities. Dogs communicate a lot through their tails. A wagging tail doesn't always mean a dog is happy; the direction and speed of the wag can convey different emotions, such as excitement, nervousness, or submission.

Dogs have an impressive short-term memory. They can learn commands and tricks quickly and remember them for an extended period. Additionally, their memory plays a role in their ability to navigate familiar environments.

Research suggests that dogs experience REM (rapid eye movement) sleep, indicating that they likely dream. You may notice your dog twitching, paddling their paws, or even making soft noises while sleeping, which could be signs of dreaming.
